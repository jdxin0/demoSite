英语4级词汇

inquire v.打听，询问

 If you inquire about something, you ask for information about it. 

He went to the hospital to inquire about her.
他到医院去打听她的情况。

Do you mind me to inquire about your salary? 

你介意我询问一下你的工资吗？

involved adj,涉及的，参与的，有关的 vt.涉及

 If you are involved in a situation or activity, you are taking part in it or have a strong connection with it. 

students involved in all forms of college athletics
参加各种大学体育运动的学生

She's involved in many extra-curricular activities .
她参加了许多课外活动.

mild adj.轻微的，不严重的，温柔的，温暖的

 Mild is used to describe something such as a feeling, attitude, or illness that is not very strong or severe.

If you have only mild symptoms, try an over-the-counter treatment.
如果症状较轻，试试用非处方药来治疗。

Anna put up a mild protest...
安娜提出了温和的抗议。

Coffee and tea are mild stimulants.
咖啡和茶是轻度兴奋剂。

 A mild person is gentle and does not get angry easily.

 Mild weather is pleasant because it is neither extremely hot nor extremely cold.

"I'm not meddling," Ken said mildly, "I'm just curious."

“我不是在管闲事，”肯温和地说，“我只是好奇。”

neglect vt/n.忽视，忽略

If you neglect someone or something, you fail to(未能) take care of them properly. 

Today's housing problems are the product of years of neglect.
今天的住房困难是多年来忽视的结果。

The fire was entirely caused by their neglect of duty.
那场火灾完全是由于他们失职而引起的。

He is chargeable with a fault or neglect.
他可能因过失或疏忽而被控告。

pace n.速度，节奏

The pace of something is the speed at which it happens or is done. 

Many people were not satisfied with the pace of change.
很多人对变革的步伐不满意。

people who prefer to live at a slower pace...
乐于放慢生活节奏的人

scene n. (给人以某种印象的)景象，场景，场面 (通过画面显示的或看到的)事件，场面，情景

It's a scene of complete devastation...
那是一副满目疮痍的景象。

Both drivers were breathalysed at the scene of the accident.
两方司机均在事故现场接受了呼气酒精检测。

innocent bystanders at the scene of the accident
事故现场的无辜旁观者

The whole place was a scene of feverish activity .
整个地方都是一片紧张匆忙的景象。

You can just picture the scene, can't you?
你完全能想象出那个画面，对吗？

on the scene 在场；出现；到场night scene 夜景new scene 新建场景；建立新场景；新场景behind the scene 幕后；幕后花絮；未开拓市场love scene n. 恋爱场面
change of scene ◎更换背景 street scene n. 街道urban scene 城市风光

shift v/n稍微移动，稍微改变

If you shift something or if it shifts, it moves slightly.

He stopped, shifting his cane to his left hand.
他停下来，把手杖移到左手。

He shifted from foot to foot.
他从一只脚换到另一只脚。

 If someone's opinion, a situation, or a policy shifts or is shifted, it changes slightly.

Attitudes to mental illness have shifted in recent years.
对精神病的态度近年来已稍微有所改变。

The emphasis should be shifted more towards Parliament.
重点应更多地转向议会。

temper n.脾气，坏脾气，暴脾气

If you refer to someone's temper or say that they have a temper, you mean that they become angry very easily. 

He needs to learn to curb his temper.
他得学着控制自己的脾气。

The boss was out of temper with him yesterday.
昨天老板对他发了脾气。

I hope he can control his temper.
我希望他能克制住自己的脾气。

urgent adj. 紧迫的；紧急的

If something is urgent, it needs to be dealt with as soon as possible. 

There is an urgent need for food and water...
现在亟需食物和水。

an urgent appeal for information
紧急呼吁提供信息

He rushed in with an urgent telex from Hong Kong.
他手里拿着一份香港发来的紧急电传冲了进来。

amuse vt.逗笑，逗乐

 If something amuses you, it makes you want to laugh or smile.

The thought seemed to amuse him...
这种想法好像把他逗乐了。

I trumped up a story to amuse the children.
我编了一个故事逗孩子们开心。

I often amuse myself with reading.
我常以读书自乐。

Amuse itself 自娱自乐Amuse Yourself 自娱自乐

appliance n. （家用）电器，器械，装置

An appliance is a device or machine in your home that you use to do a job such as cleaning or cooking. Appliances are often electrical.

He could also learn to use the vacuum cleaner, the washing machine and other household appliances.
他可以再学学使用吸尘器、洗衣机和其他家用电器。

Do not use appliance for other than intended use.
请不要将设备用于除说明书上标明的其他用途。

arouse vt. 引起（反应或态度） ，激起（感情或直觉）

 If something arouses a particular reaction or attitude in people, it causes them to have that reaction or attitude.

We left in the daytime so as not to arouse suspicion.
我们在白天离开以避免引起怀疑。

His revolutionary work in linguistics has aroused intense scholarly interest.
他在语言学方面的革命性著作引起了强烈的学术兴趣。

 If something arouses a particular feeling or instinct that exists in someone, it causes them to experience that feeling or instinct strongly.

There is nothing like a long walk to arouse the appetite...
没有什么比走很长的路更能激起食欲的了。

He aroused her mothering instincts.
他激发了她母性的本能。

barrier n.障碍物，障碍

A barrier is something such as a rule, law, or policy that makes it difficult or impossible for something to happen or be achieved.

Lack of confidence is the biggest barrier to investment in the region.
缺乏信心是在这一地区投资的最大障碍。

The lorry went into a skid and crashed into the barrier.
卡车失控打滑撞到障碍物上。

The angry crowds would not keep back the barrier.
愤怒的人群不肯站在栅栏后面。

conceal vt.隐藏，隐瞒(信息)，掩饰(情感)，掩盖

 If you conceal something, you cover it or hide it carefully.

Five people were arrested for carrying concealed weapons.
5人因身上藏有武器被捕。

If you conceal a piece of information or a feeling, you do not let other people know about it.

obert could not conceal his relief...
罗伯特无法掩饰自己如释重负的心情。

She knew at once that he was concealing something from her.
她马上知道了他有事瞒着她。

If something conceals something else, it covers it and prevents it from being seen.

The hat concealed her hair.
帽子遮住了她的头发。

He couldn't conceal his envy of me.
他掩饰不住对我的忌妒。

He had to conceal his identity to escape the police.
为了躲避警方，他只好隐瞒身份。

He tried to conceal his instinctive revulsion at the idea.
他试图饰盖自己对这一想法本能的厌恶。

cope vi.(妥善的)处理，(成功的)应付 n.长袍

If you cope with a problem or task, you deal with it successfully. 

It was amazing how my mother coped with bringing up three children on less than thirty dollars a week.
让人惊叹的是我妈妈怎样以每周不到30美元的开销养大了3个孩子。

The problems were an annoyance, but we managed to cope.
这些问题很讨厌，但是我们都设法解决了。

Doctors are having to cope with an everexpanding workload.
医生不得不应付日益增加的工作量。

deliver vt. 递送;投递;运送 , 作(讲座);发表(讲话)

If you deliver something somewhere, you take it there.

The Canadians plan to deliver more food to southern Somalia...
加拿大计划向索马里南部地区运送更多的食品。

The spy returned to deliver a second batch of classified documents...
那名间谍返回来递送第二批机密文件。

We were told the pizza would be delivered in 20 minutes.
我们被告知比萨饼 20 分钟后送到。

If you deliver a lecture or speech, you give it in public.

The president will deliver a speech about schools...
总统将就学校问题发表讲话。

discipline n.纪律，(尤指大学里的)学科

Discipline is the practice of making people obey rules or standards of behaviour, and punishing them when they do not.

discipline problems in the classroom.
课堂纪律问题.

The school has a reputation for high standards of discipline.
这所学校因纪律严格而名闻遐迩。

 A discipline is a particular area of study, especially a subject of study in a college or university.

You've got to make sure that people work together across disciplines...
你必须确保让那些出身不同学科的人一起共事。
We're looking for people from a wide range of disciplines.
我们需要各个学科的人才。

dispute n.争论，争执，纠纷

A dispute is an argument or disagreement between people or groups.

They have won previous pay disputes with the government.
他们曾赢过前几起与政府的工资纠纷。

The matter in dispute is the ownership of the house.
眼下的纠纷是这所房子的所有权问题。

The dispute at the moment revolved about the question of whom to send.
当时的争论是围绕着派谁去好这一问题而进行的。

dissolve vt.(使)溶解，解散(团体或机构，会议)

If a substance dissolves in liquid or if you dissolve it, it becomes mixed with the liquid and disappears. 

Heat gently until the sugar dissolves...
慢慢加热直到糖溶解为止。

Dissolve the salt in a little boiled water.
将盐溶解在少许开水中。

 When an organization or institution is dissolved, it is officially ended or broken up.

  • The committee has been dissolved...

    委员会已经解散了。

essential adj.至关重要的，必不可少的 n.必需品

Something that is essential is extremely important or absolutely necessary to a particular subject, situation, or activity. 

It was absolutely essential to separate crops from the areas that animals used as pasture.
把庄稼与牲畜的放牧区分开至关重要。

I do not know whether you realize it, but this condition is essential to us.
我不知道你是否了解，但是，这个条件对我们是必要的。

The essentials are the things that are absolutely necessary for the situation you are in or for the task you are doing.
